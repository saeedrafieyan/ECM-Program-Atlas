Current Findings

1. Human Matrisome genes were successfully matched to HPA expression data.
   - 1009 out of 1027 Matrisome genes were matched.
   - Only 18 Matrisome genes were missing.

2. ECM-only tissue expression preserves biological structure.
   - CNS tissues cluster together.
   - Immune and lymphoid tissues show coherent grouping.
   - Digestive, reproductive, epithelial, and stromal tissues show partial structure.

3. Matrisome genes outperform matched random non-ECM genes for several global metrics.
   - Stronger PCA PC1 and PC1+PC2 variance.
   - Better silhouette scores.
   - Mixed results for nearest-neighbor and clustering metrics.

4. Matrisome category analysis showed category-specific biological signals.
   - Core matrisome and ECM glycoproteins show strong global structure.
   - Collagens and proteoglycans capture specialized structural axes.
   - Secreted factors and ECM regulators sometimes align with clustering but may reflect signaling or cell-type composition.

5. Cross-dataset reproducibility analysis supports the robustness of the signal.
   - Core matrisome and ECM glycoproteins were the most reproducible feature sets.
   - The strongest reproducible metrics were PCA PC1+PC2 variance and silhouette in PCA10 space.

Main conclusion:
Structural Matrisome categories, especially core matrisome and ECM glycoproteins, reproducibly encode low-dimensional biological organization across human tissues, brain regions, and cell types.
